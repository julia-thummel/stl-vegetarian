Cafe Gratitude

Larchmont, Los Angeles

This place is super cute on the inside- it has little air plants and carafes of water you might find at some european cafe table. The food is no compromise for cute atmosphere though. They also have great drinks, like rose lemonade.

Sage Organic Vegan Bistro

Echo Park, Los Angeles

Sage is a completely vegan restaurant with a bit of a brazillian flare, if you're looking to get food with lots of flavor. They also have catering services if you'd like to cater to your next vegan rager, but they're better as a sit-down restaurant.

Try this:

Poor Boy Sandwich

Grain Caf√©

Mid-City, Los Angeles

Most everything on the menu is vegan or vegetarian, but be careful because there are a couple sandwiches with fish in them. Other than that, this place is great for lunch, and offers a wide variety of vegan deserts that keep customers coming back.

Try this:

Delux Burger

Shojin Downtown

Little Tokyo, Los Angeles

This is a great restaurant for a sit down meal, whether it be a casual night out with friends or a more formal date night. It's right in the heart of L.A's Little Tokyo, so it's full of authentic Japanese cuisine catered towards a vegetarian/vegan audience.

Try this:

Dynamite Roll

check it out!

Rasika

Penn Quarter, Washington D.C.

If you consider yourself a bougie vegetarian, or perhaps just looking for a fancy evening out in the D.C. area, then Rasika is your place to be. It's Indian cuisine, so there are some meat dishes, but overall very veggie-friendly.

Try this:

Matar Paneer

check it out!

Triple B Fresh

Dupont Circle, Washington D.C.

Right in the center of the lovely Dupont circle you'll find this gem of a restaurant, Triple B Fresh. They serve a huge variety of korean staples, some of which have meat in them so be careful! You know the drill, fam.

black rice with tofu

Keren Restaurant

Dupont Circle, Washington D.C.

If you're in the mood for some cheap eats, but also want a little culture and flavor, Keren Restaurant is sure to deliver. Some dishes do have meat, but you don't have to try too hard to find something sans meat but full of flavor.

Feeling a little creative? Why not go create your own pizza at &pizza! It's great because you don't have to specify you don't want meat on your pizza. It's also super cheap and the pizzas come in fun long shapes.
